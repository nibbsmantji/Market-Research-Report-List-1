<p><p>The ice cream market consists of two primary types: soft ice cream and hard ice cream. Soft ice cream, known for its smooth texture and lighter consistency, is typically served at a slightly warmer temperature, allowing it to melt faster and create a creamy mouthfeel. In contrast, hard ice cream is churned at a lower temperature, resulting in a denser and firmer product. Both variants cater to different consumer preferences, with soft ice cream often found in desserts and parlors, while hard ice cream is popular for at-home consumption and premium brands.</p></p>
