<p><p>The graphic design software market comprises two primary types: pixel-based image editors and vector-based image editors. Pixel-based image editors, like Adobe Photoshop, manipulate raster images composed of pixels, making them ideal for detailed photo editing and complex textures. In contrast, vector-based image editors, such as Adobe Illustrator, utilize mathematical equations to create graphics, allowing for scalable designs without loss of quality. Each type serves distinct purposes, catering to different design needs, from digital painting to logo creation and typography.</p></p>
