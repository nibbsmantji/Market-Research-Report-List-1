<p><p>The household textile market comprises two main types: non-woven and woven textiles. Non-woven textiles are created by bonding fibers together using heat, chemicals, or mechanical methods, resulting in materials often used for disposable products like cleaning wipes and bed linens. In contrast, woven textiles are constructed by interlacing threads at right angles, offering durability and a wide range of designs for products such as curtains, bedspreads, and upholstery. Both markets cater to diverse consumer needs and preferences in home decor and functionality.</p></p>
