<p><p>The compostable packaging materials market consists of two primary types: compostable plastics and compostable paper. Compostable plastics are typically made from renewable resources such as starch or polylactic acid (PLA), designed to break down in industrial composting facilities. On the other hand, compostable paper is produced from natural fibers and is biodegradable, facilitating decomposition. Both types aim to reduce environmental impact by offering sustainable alternatives to conventional packaging, promoting circular economy practices, and enhancing waste management systems.</p></p>
